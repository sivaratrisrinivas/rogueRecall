## Method and evidence boundary

The roster deliberately uses English Wikisource/Wikimedia source pages rather
than commercial ebook or lyric aggregators. The cited work pages expose the
edition text, publication metadata, source scan where available, revision
history, and an explicit Wikimedia rights tag. Wikimedia's
[PD-old-100-expired template](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Template:PD-old-100-expired)
states the relevant rule: publication before 1931 and an author dead for at
least 100 years. Wikimedia also documents
[permanent links](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Help:Permanent_link) and the
[revision API](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/API:Revisions), including revision
IDs and SHA-1 values.

For intake, the live work link below is a discovery and evidence page, not the
immutable locator. The contributor must:

1. choose the exact scan-backed edition shown on the work page;
2. save the scan bytes and their SHA-256;
3. save the work/index/file pages and the permanent revision IDs of every
   transcluded page used by the excerpt (the revision API is the immutable
   revision mechanism);
4. preserve the page's rights template, author death date, title page,
   publication page, and any editor/illustrator credits; and
5. have the rights reviewer confirm that no later introduction, translation,
   illustration, arrangement, or other edition contribution enters the chosen
   reference excerpt.

This is intentionally stricter than relying on the CC BY-SA footer for
Wikisource's database/page presentation. The proposed Source Works rely on the
underlying work's claimed worldwide-public-domain status, not on a claim that
Wikisource can relicense third-party text. Wikimedia itself cautions that
[reusers remain responsible for checking copyright](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Commons:Reusing_content_outside_Wikimedia).

## Release blockers and review queue

All 34 rows are deliberately **unaccepted**. Before any becomes an Evaluation
Case, the contributor and reviewers must resolve at least these items:

- freeze exact scan/text bytes and SHA-256 values instead of retaining live URLs;
- independently confirm the worldwide-PD conclusion for the chosen edition,
  including posthumous-publication and country-of-origin rules;
- identify every lyricist, adapter, translator, editor, arranger, illustrator,
  and other contributor and determine whether their contribution enters the
  reference material;
- verify the claimed first-publication date and source-supported category/genre;
- create the case-specific Rights Evidence and excerpt-limit calculation;
- obtain the Contributor Attestation, independent case review, rights-reviewer
  acceptance, and release-curator approval only after the actual case exists;
  and
- record exclusions if a candidate fails any check; substitution is safer than
  stretching an uncertain rights theory.
